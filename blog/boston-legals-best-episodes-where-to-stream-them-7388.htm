Boston Legal’s Best Episodes: Where to Stream Them

Few legal dramas have managed to blend sharp wit, social commentary, and courtroom theatrics as brilliantly as Boston Legal. Created by David E. Kelley, this spin-off of The Practice delivered some of television’s most memorable legal battles, often tackling controversial issues with humor and heart. Even years after its finale, the show remains relevant, especially in today’s politically charged climate.

If you’re looking to revisit the series or discover it for the first time, here’s a guide to its best episodes—and where you can stream them.

The Top 5 Must-Watch Episodes

1. "The Black Widow" (Season 1, Episode 7)

This early standout introduces Catherine Piper (Betty White), a wealthy widow accused of murdering her husbands. The episode’s dark humor and biting commentary on ageism and gender stereotypes still resonate today. Alan Shore’s (James Spader) closing argument is a masterclass in legal theatrics.

Where to Stream: Hulu, Amazon Prime Video

2. "Schadenfreude" (Season 2, Episode 2)

Alan Shore defends a man who killed a child molester, forcing the jury (and viewers) to confront vigilante justice. The episode’s moral ambiguity is especially timely in an era where social media often fuels public outrage before due process.

Where to Stream: Hulu, Peacock

3. "The Nutcrackers" (Season 3, Episode 7)

Denny Crane (William Shatner) and Alan take on a case involving a woman fired for being "too sexy." The episode skewers workplace discrimination and corporate hypocrisy—a topic that’s only grown more relevant with movements like #MeToo.

Where to Stream: Hulu, Apple TV

4. "Tortured Souls" (Season 4, Episode 11)

One of the show’s most politically charged episodes, "Tortured Souls" tackles Guantanamo Bay detainees and government-sanctioned torture. Given ongoing debates about human rights and national security, this episode feels ripped from today’s headlines.

Where to Stream: Hulu, Peacock

5. "Last Call" (Season 5, Episode 13)

The series finale is a bittersweet farewell, with Alan and Denny debating same-sex marriage on the balcony—a fitting end for a show that never shied away from progressive stances.

Where to Stream: Hulu, Amazon Prime Video

Why Boston Legal Still Matters Today

A Mirror to Modern Society

From gun control to climate change, Boston Legal tackled issues that remain hot-button topics. The show’s willingness to address systemic flaws—often through Alan Shore’s fiery monologues—makes it eerily prescient.

The Power of Satire

Few shows balanced drama and satire as deftly. Whether mocking corporate greed or political corruption, Boston Legal used humor to expose uncomfortable truths—a tactic that feels especially necessary in today’s polarized world.

Where to Binge the Full Series

If you’re ready to dive in, here’s where you can stream Boston Legal in its entirety:
- Hulu (All seasons)
- Amazon Prime Video (Purchase required)
- Peacock (Select seasons)

Whether you’re in it for the legal drama, the social commentary, or just Denny Crane’s legendary one-liners, Boston Legal remains a must-watch. Happy streaming!
